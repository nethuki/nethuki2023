Threats to Turtles

Turtles face many threats in their natural habitats. Here are three of the main threats:

  • Pollution in the oceans is a major threat to turtles. It can cause illness and death in turtles, and also damage their habitats. Plastic waste is one of the biggest sources of pollution, and turtles often mistake plastic for food, leading to serious health problems. To prevent pollution, we can reduce our use of single-use plastics and properly dispose of all waste.

    Learn more

  • Turtles face many predators, such as birds, raccoons, and dogs. These predators often destroy turtle nests and consume the eggs and hatchlings. To prevent predation, we can monitor and protect turtle nests, and reduce the impact of predators on turtle populations.

    Learn more

  • Commercial and recreational fishing activities can harm turtles by accidentally catching them in nets and lines. This is known as bycatch, and can cause serious injury or death to turtles. To prevent bycatch, we can use alternative fishing gear that is less harmful to turtles, and regulate fishing practices to minimize turtle mortality.
